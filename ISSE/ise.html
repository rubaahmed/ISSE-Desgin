About Cyber ​​Information Scurity Engineering

The rapid technological development, particularly in the area of information and communication technology, has led to the wide application and proliferation of computerized systems. Given the importance of information and the urgent need to keep it from being hacked for the purpose of change or theft, one of themost important and necessary science in this area has emerged:
information security science, which protects information from the risks it poses or the barrier to attack it by providing the tools and means to provide it.
Standards and measures taken to prevent access to information by unauthorized persons
through communications and to ensure the authenticity and validity of such communications.

That is why there is an urgent need in our various institutions that use modern technology, computerized systems and local and global information networks for information security engineers to design, oversee and implement information systems security systems in a highly efficient, successful and inexpensive manner.

Objectives of specialization

To take note of modern information security techniques and apply them using appropriate means to ensure that the objectives of individuals and institutions are achieved.

Design and management of secure information technology resources for individuals and institutions.

To keep pace with variables in the area of security and protection of computerized information and
the ability to assess access to modern technologies.

Knowledge of the theoretical rules and mathematical foundations on which information technology is based

Admission to the major

  • A student has obtained a general secondary degree from the scientific branch at a rate of 75% and above.
  • The student's average diploma is 75%, and the comprehensive examination certificate is well rated.

Work fields

The Information Security Engineer works in various local and international institutions that apply computerized information systems and need to design or implement their information security systems in order to protect them.

  • Also work in development teams for security information systems.
  • Researcher at research and related studies centres.
